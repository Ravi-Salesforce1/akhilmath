1) \$112 in 8 hours

Teacher: Find dollars per hour (unit rate).

Formula \(\displaystyle \text{Unit rate}=\frac{\text{total money}}{\text{hours}}\)
Student Steps
  1. Write fraction: \(\dfrac{112}{8}\) dollars/hour.
  2. Divide: \(112\div 8=14\).
  3. Attach unit: \(14\) dollars/hour \(\Rightarrow \$14.00/\text{hour}\).
Final Answer: \(\$14.00/\text{hour}\)
Check: \(14\times 8=112\). ✓

23) \$4.99 for 6 cans or \$7.99 for 10 cans

Teacher: Compare unit prices (dollars per can) and choose the lower price.

Formula \(\displaystyle \text{unit price}=\frac{\$}{\text{cans}},\quad \frac{4.99}{6}\) vs. \(\frac{7.99}{10}\).
Student Steps
  1. Compute first: \(4.99\div 6\approx 0.8317\Rightarrow \$0.83/\text{can}\) (rounded).
  2. Compute second: \(7.99\div 10=0.799\Rightarrow \$0.80/\text{can}\) (rounded).
  3. Compare: \(\$0.80<\$0.83\Rightarrow\) better deal is \$7.99 for 10 cans.
Final Answer: \(\$7.99\) for \(10\) cans (lower unit price)
Check: \(0.80\times 10=\$8.00\approx \$7.99\) (rounding), and \(0.83\times 6\approx \$4.98\approx \$4.99\). ✓
24) \$21.50 for 4 lb or \$15.10 for 3 lb

Teacher: Compare dollars per pound and choose the lower unit price.

Formula \(\displaystyle \text{unit price}=\frac{\$}{\text{lb}},\quad \frac{21.50}{4}\) vs. \(\frac{15.10}{3}\).
Student Steps
  1. First option: \(21.50\div 4=5.375\Rightarrow \$5.38/\text{lb}\) (rounded).
  2. Second option: \(15.10\div 3=5.033\ldots\Rightarrow \$5.03/\text{lb}\) (rounded).
  3. Compare: \(\$5.03<\$5.38\Rightarrow\) choose \$15.10 for 3 lb.
Final Answer: \(\$15.10\) for \(3\) pounds (lower unit price)
Check: \(5.03\times 3\approx \$15.09\approx \$15.10\); \(5.38\times 4\approx \$21.52\approx \$21.50\). ✓
